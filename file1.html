Executive Summary

A comprehensive analysis of recent academic literature, frameworks, and industry whitepapers (2021-2025) detailing the evolution and architecture of Digital Twin (DT) systems.

The Evolution of the Digital Twin

Historically, Digital Twins emerged as isolated, domain-specific solutions (e.g., a specific predictive maintenance model for a single turbine). The analyzed documents reveal a paradigm shift: the industry is aggressively moving towards standardized Reference Architectures (RAs).

Key frameworks like TwinArch, the makeTwin software platform, and the Digital Twin Consortium's Platform Stack emphasize interoperability, open-source collaboration, and rigorous, multi-view architectural documentation (ISO 42010). The integration of the Cloud-Edge Continuum is now a fundamental requirement, allowing heavy simulation in the cloud while retaining low-latency control at the physical edge.

Cloud-Edge Continuum

The state of the art dictates a hybrid deployment. High-fidelity rendering and heavy ML training occur in the Cloud. Time-sensitive data aggregation, filtering, and immediate control loops execute at the Edge. The architecture must dynamically balance workloads across this continuum.
